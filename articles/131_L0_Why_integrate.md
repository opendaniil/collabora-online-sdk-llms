By integrating Collabora Online into your services, you provide your users with a comprehensive solution for collaborative document editing, while being gentle on your hardware resources and budget.

The easy solution is to go with an existing integration with an Enterprise File Synchronization and Sharing (EFSS). Systems like NextCloud and ownCloud offer out of the box support for Collabora Online. You can check a list of [available integrations](084_L0_Available_integrations.md).

If your custom information system calls for having office documents attached, integrating with Collabora Online can provide an extra level of functionality.

If, as a vendor, your solution calls for viewing or editing office documents, integrating Collabora Online can be the cost effective way to provide the functionality to your customers.

Collabora Online’s use of the WOPI protocol and its extensive support for Microsoft Office formats make it a good candidate as a replacement for existing office document editing solutions, with a minimal variance in functionality.

Another advantage is that you get to choose where Collabora Online is hosted. You can host it on-premises, in your own data center, in the cloud next to your existing solutions, or even through a provider that offers the service of hosting it for you. In each and every cases, you get to decide which supplier you use and how you get to comply with regulations like GDPR. It’s a path to digital sovereignty.

This section will guide you and lay down what you need to know to integrate Collabora Online within your solution, by developing an integration.
